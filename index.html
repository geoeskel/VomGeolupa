What is a 'Working Dog'?

First, I want to say a “workings, line dog” is called so based on its genetics. It is based on its pedigree and NOT it’s titles. There are those who think that a dog can’t be called a working line dog if it has not been tested or scored. There is nothing further from the truth!
When an average person thinks of working dogs, they think of dark pigmented dogs, such as black, dark sable, sable and blanketed back dogs. The term working lines is considered now to be any dog that is not “Show line”. There are working line trainers, handlers, and enthusiast who get very offensive when you speak of a working line German Shepherd who is not titled or being titled in a sport discipline. These are people who are only capable of seeing the dog industry, (and it is a very large industry) through a small window of relevance to themselves. In order to present themselves as a superior dog person, they find satisfaction in judging all other dogs against the SPORT dogs.
I want to make it clear that I sincerely appreciate and respect those who are committed to training and trialing their dogs in Sport disciplines. These dedicated owners and trainers provide the industry with a reminder and demonstration of the abilities of a real working dog. They spend hours, days, months and years perfecting their dogs and it is to be commended. For that reason, we should allow their constructive criticism when done in the right spirit. Taking criticism from a feather is a lot easier than criticism from a hammer. Over 30 years life in dog world has taught me so,
In summary, in this REAL world, less than 1% of German Shepherd dogs are titled. There are several levels of titles requiring different levels of training. There are confirmation titles, rally titles, and sport titles. There are also certifications such a Personal Perfection certification. Just remember, the dog doesn’t realize these titles or certifications. The dog only knows the joy of satisfying you as an owner, and responds to the love you show him.
