You and your dog. What is it all about?

It is all about the relationship you have with the dog. The dog has to see you as a part of the pack, the leader. The dog is not just a tool. It is all about teaching the dog PROPERLY, identifying and targeting the enemy which is the man and not the fabric of the equipment. It is about making the dog be your partner and not to be in conflict with what you want,It is about the dog working for you and with you and not for toys and treats. It is about teaching the dog from the get-go to target the man and NEVER EVER THE equipment, and I mean from day one. It is about building the default where the dog works best when he/she does what he/she loves the most and reverts to what he/she learned first when under stress. It is about never teaching a dog some fake purpose of the training (Like sit for a treat). In attack, the dog is ALWAYS from DAY ONE taught to target the man and NEVER anything made out of fabric...EVER. It is about teaching the dog how to fight and not how to and where to bite. It is about teaching the dog what is the mission and that he and I are in it together and when the mission is accomplished and when I say is over and the dog defuses and is not merely under control but is defused which means he is in the same state of mind as the handler, he is being praised by me by pet and voice and happy attitude. And it is about the dog being rewarded by me and my kind words and pet on the head and not by a toy or treat. I am not a dispenser of treats but I am the leader of the pack of 2, and to the dog, I am the measure of all things, and me being happy with the dog's performance is the dog's reward. I train dogs for well over 30 years and I have learned that this is the way to get the best results. But that is just me.

What is a 'working dog'?

A “working line dog” is so called based on its genetics. It is based on its pedigree and NOT its titles. There are those who think that a dog can’t be called a working line dog if it has not been tested or scored. There is nothing further from the truth!

'Fight Drive'. What exactly is it?

Fight drive. What is it? There are many definitions of what is a fight drive. This leads to confusion and even arguments based on a lack of clarity on what these terms mean. And I am all about clarity. I think we should unite our definition of this term or at least understand what other people mean when they use this term and there are different understandings to this term. The definition or the understanding of this very important term needs to be concise, short, clear, and logical. Today I have had a discussion about it with a man whom I can only RESPECT, yet we disagreed on this topic. He, same as many, is basically equating fight drive with defence drive. I personally do not subscribe to such a ( common) definition of "fight drive". IMO these two terms - "defence drive" and "fight drive" - are different for a reason. They, in my opinion, mean 2 different things. To me, the fight drive does not stand on its own but describes the characteristics of a fight during prey or defensive attack. Thus the fight may be done in prey or defence. Thus to me, fight drive is a "subsection" of prey or defence action conducted by a dog. I like to say that fight drive describes "the ability and commitment of the dog to bring the fight to his enemy - in case of defence drive or to his prey - in case of prey drive." that is my definition. It can further be described by the amount of energy expelled during action per unit of time (again in prey or defence). I hope this is as clear as mud. :)
